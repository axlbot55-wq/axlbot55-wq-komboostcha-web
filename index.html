What is kombucha?
Kombucha is fermented tea — brewed with a live culture called a SCOBY. It's naturally fizzy, lightly tart, and contains live beneficial bacteria.
What does it taste like?
Think slightly effervescent iced tea with a gentle tang. Not vinegary, not overly sweet. Every batch has natural variation — that's part of what makes it handcrafted.
Is it safe? It's fermented at home?
Each batch is brewed in a clean, dedicated setup with careful handling and fresh bottling. Fermentation and natural acidity are part of what makes kombucha stable — but if you have specific health concerns, message us on WhatsApp before ordering.
How do I store it?
Keep it refrigerated once you receive it. It stays fresh for 2–3 weeks chilled. Drink within a few days of opening.
What about the Greek yoghurt?
Full-fat milk cultured overnight and strained — thick, creamy, no thickeners or additives. Best consumed within 5–7 days. Keep refrigerated.

How we make it

1

Source the culture

A live SCOBY (symbiotic culture of bacteria and yeast) is the heart of every batch — it's what ferments the tea into kombucha.

2

First fermentation

Brewed tea and raw sugar feed the culture for 7–10 days in our keg, producing that naturally tart, lightly fizzy base.

3

Bottle & condition

Bottled in 500ml glass bottles and left to naturally carbonate for 2–3 more days before delivery.

4

Greek yoghurt strain

Full-fat milk cultured overnight and then strained to give that thick, protein-rich texture. No thickeners, ever.

FAQ

Where do you deliver?
General QC and Ortigas only for now. If you're just outside the zone, message us — we'll see what we can do.
How does ordering work?
Submit the order form on the Order tab. We'll WhatsApp you within a few hours to confirm your order and arrange delivery timing.
When do you brew and deliver?
We brew in small batches — typically one keg every 1–2 weeks. The order form shows what's available from the current batch. Order by Friday midnight for Sunday delivery when available.
How long does the kombucha stay fresh?
2–3 weeks refrigerated. Keep it cold from the moment you receive it. The live cultures continue to slowly ferment in the bottle — it gets slightly tangier over time, which is completely normal.
Is it sweet or sour?
Lightly tart with a gentle natural sweetness. Not as sweet as store-bought, not as sharp as vinegar. First-timers usually find it easy to enjoy straight from the bottle over ice.
Can I return or exchange?
We stand behind every batch. If something is wrong with your order, message us on WhatsApp immediately and we'll sort it out.
Can I order larger quantities?
Batches are small by design — usually 18–24 bottles. For bulk orders (6+ bottles), message us before the batch is ready and we'll reserve yours first.
